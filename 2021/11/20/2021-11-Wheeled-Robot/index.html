Intelligent Visual Perception Robot

Created2021-11-20|Updated2023-12-02
|Post View:

(Third-Person)

(Egocentric)

Code & Design Document

This project participated in the National University Contest on Intelligent Robotic Innovations(a robot fighting conetst). The contest was about two wheeled robots battling in an arena. The goal of the contest was to push the rival robot out of the arena, avoid obstacles and the opponent’s attack. Because of the COVID-19, the rules of the contest changed. The temporary rule needed robots to detect more blocks and push them out while not falling down the arena.

I was responsible for the software part of our robot, mostly on motion control and object detection. The robot perceived the surrounding environment through sensors and a camera. Through sensors, the robot can measure the distance between the enemy, obstacles, or the arena’s edge and itself. Through the camera, it can use all the CV methods to perceive. Some of the blocks were painted on April Tag, while others were not. To detect all blocks, I deployed the advanced object detection model YOLOv5 to the raspberry pi of our robot. By analyzing the perceived information, our robot can know how to move and choose the proper attack strategy.

Through our efforts, Our team had achieved a very good result. Finally, We won the national first prize(Rank: 5 / 253).
